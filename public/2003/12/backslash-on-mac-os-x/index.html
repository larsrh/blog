Backslash on Mac OS X

Stefan Tilkov, Dec 23, 2003

I don’t know what imbecile at Apple created the German keymap for a PowerBook running Mac OS X, but after just having wasted about half an hour of my life with trying to find a way to enter a backslash (‘') on this damn thing, I found out that it’s Shift-Alt-7. Should’ve been obvious? Right.

On July 20, 2005 11:19 PM, chucker said:

As someone pointed out, the reason backslash is on option-shift-7 is that all three vertical line symbols are on the 7 — slash on shift-7, pipe on option-7, and backslash (a combination of the two, so to speak) on option-shift-7.

As for programming, the German layout — be it the Mac variant or the typical IBM PC one — has never been efficient for that. Use a US English keyboard, it has the tilde, backslash, semicolon and whatnot much more accessible. On a Mac, the US English keyboard also makes it somewhat easy to access umlauts — option-U+(a|o|u) — , the sz — option-S — , etc.
